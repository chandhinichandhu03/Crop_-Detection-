### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Sclerotinia Stem Rot (White Mold) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Sclerotinia sclerotiorum*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked lesions on lower stems and branches; bleached, straw-colored stem tissue
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Thiram-treated seeds, adequate plant spacing, crop rotation
  * **Chemical**: Thiophanate-methyl (1g/L), Boscalid (0.5g/L), Iprodione, Fluazinam at early flowering
  * **Prevention**: Crop rotation (non-host crops for 3+ years), avoid dense planting, improve drainage

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### White Grubs (Chafer Beetles) (*Holotrichia consanguinea, Leucopholis lepidophora (multiple spp.)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Plants wilt suddenly (root destruction by grubs); patches of dead plants in field; soil becomes soft and easy to pull plants (roots eaten); adult beetles feed on tree leaves at night
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Metarhizium anisopliae fungus (1×10⁹ spores/ml) soil application; ploughing to expose and destroy grubs; light traps for adults; neem cake 250 kg/ha
  * **Chemical**: Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (5L/ha) soil incorporation before planting; Carbofuran 3G (25 kg/ha); Phorate 10G; Imidacloprid seed treatment
  * **Biological**: Metarhizium anisopliae, Beauveria bassiana soil inoculants; Heterorhabditis bacteriophora (entomopathogenic nematode)

#### Spider Mite (Two-Spotted) (*Tetranychus urticae*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Stippled (tiny pale dots/punctures) on leaves from below; leaves turn bronze/yellow and dry; fine silk webbing; premature defoliation
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Water spray to wash off; neem oil 5ml/L; wettable sulfur 3g/L; insecticidal soap; predatory mites release; soap solution sprays
  * **Chemical**: Abamectin 1.9% EC (0.5ml/L), Spiromesifen 22.9% SC (1ml/L), Hexythiazox 5% EC (1ml/L), Bifenazate, Clofentezine, Fenpyroximate
  * **Biological**: Phytoseiulus persimilis (excellent predator), Amblyseius californicus, Neoseiulus californicus, Feltiella acarisuga (gall midge)
