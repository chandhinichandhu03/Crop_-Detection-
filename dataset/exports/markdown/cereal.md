## Barley

**Scientific Name**: *Hordeum vulgare* L.
**IUCN Conservation Status**: `LC`

### 📋 Botanical Taxonomy Hierarchy

| Rank | Name | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kingdom** | Plantae | Plant kingdom |
| **Division** | Tracheophyta | Vascular/Non-vascular division |
| **Class** | Liliopsida | Class classification |
| **Order** | Poales | Order grouping |
| **Family** | Poaceae | Grass family; cereals and grains - most economically important plant family |
| **Genus** | Hordeum | Genus grouping |
| **Species** | *vulgare* | Species epithet |

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Rice Weevil (*Sitophilus oryzae*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Grain kernels hollowed out; dust and frass in stored grain; characteristic exit holes; grain losses 20-40%; quality deterioration
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Clean dry grain storage (< 14% moisture); diatomaceous earth 1.5g/kg mixed with grain; bay leaves in storage; neem leaf powder mixed with grain; hermetic storage (PICS bags, metal silos)
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation (Aluminium phosphide tablets 3 tablets/tonne); Pyrethrin spray on empty stores; Deltamethrin WP on wall surfaces; Pirimiphos-methyl admixture
  * **Biological**: No practical biological control for storage pests

#### Lesser Grain Borer (*Rhyzopertha dominica*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Flour dust from feeding activity; grain kernels bored from outside; 'rice dust'; severe infestations cause 30-50% losses; heat generation from infested grain
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Hermetic storage, diatomaceous earth, proper drying below 12% moisture
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation, Pirimiphos-methyl grain protectant
  * **Biological**: Limited; Theocolax elegans (ectoparasitoid wasp)

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Powdery Mildew (Wheat) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Blumeria graminis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: White fluffy powdery patches on upper leaf surface of lower leaves
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur (3g/L), potassium bicarbonate
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Triadimefon 25% WP (1g/L), Azoxystrobin
  * **Prevention**: Resistant varieties, avoid dense planting, balanced nitrogen, timely fungicide sprays

#### Stripe Rust (Yellow Rust) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Stripe of yellow-orange uredia arranged in rows along leaf veins on upper leaf surface
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur preventively; no effective organic cure
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Tebuconazole 250 EW (1ml/L), Azoxystrobin + Propiconazole
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties (HD-3226, WH1105), timely sowing to escape high-risk period, prophylactic fungicide at flag leaf stage

#### Loose Smut (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Ustilago tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Diseased plants emerge earlier; infected ear with black spore mass instead of grain
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Hot water seed treatment (52°C for 10 minutes)
  * **Chemical**: Carboxin + Thiram seed treatment (2g/kg seed), Tebuconazole seed treatment
  * **Prevention**: Use certified smut-free seeds, systemic seed treatment fungicides, plant resistant varieties

#### Stem Rust (Black Rust) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Puccinia graminis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Brick-red oval to elongated blisters (pustules) on stems and leaves that rupture releasing reddish-brown powdery spores
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur; remove barberry bushes from fields
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Tebuconazole (1ml/L), at first sign of infection
  * **Prevention**: Rust-resistant varieties, eradicate barberry, prophylactic fungicide in Ug99-risk areas

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Gray Leaf Spot (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Cercospora zeae-maydis*
- **Symptoms**: Small, irregular tan spots with yellow halos, mainly on lower leaves
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper-based sprays preventively
  * **Chemical**: Azoxystrobin, Propiconazole, Pyraclostrobin at tasseling stage
  * **Prevention**: Hybrid resistance, crop rotation, tillage to bury debris, fungicide at VT/R1

#### Northern Corn Leaf Blight (NCLB) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Exserohilum turcicum (Setosphaeria turcica)*
- **Symptoms**: Long (5-15cm) elliptical tan to grayish-green lesions with wavy margins on lower leaves
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper-based fungicides preventively
  * **Chemical**: Azoxystrobin + Propiconazole (Quilt Xcel 1L/ha), Propiconazole alone (1ml/L)
  * **Prevention**: Resistant hybrids, crop rotation, remove infected debris, timely planting

#### Maize Lethal Necrosis (MLN) (Viral)
- **Pathogen**: *Maize chlorotic mottle virus (MCMV) + Potyviruses*
- **Symptoms**: Chlorotic (yellow) streaks and mottling on younger leaves; pale green to yellow leaf discoloration
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: No cure; control insect vectors with neem oil, reflective mulch
  * **Chemical**: No direct treatment; insecticides for vector control (Imidacloprid, Thiamethoxam)
  * **Prevention**: Plant MLN-tolerant/resistant varieties, control thrips and aphids, crop rotation, early planting, remove infected plants

### 🌾 Crop Cultivation & Agronomy Guide

- **Origin**: Southeast Asia (China-India border region)
- **Climatic Preference**: Tropical and subtropical (Temp: 21-37°C (germination 18-40°C), Rainfall: 1000-2000mm annual; most during growing season)
- **Soil Requirements**: Heavy clay or clay-loam soils with good water retention (pH: 5.0-7.0 (optimal 6.0-6.5))
  *Special Note: Impermeable clay layer needed for paddy cultivation; good drainage for upland rice*
- **Sowing Details**: Seed rate: Transplanted: 25-30 kg/ha; Direct seeded: 80-100 kg/ha, Spacing: 20×15 cm or 20×20 cm; 2-3 seedlings per hill
- **Irrigation Needs**: Method: Continuous flooding (5-7cm standing water) in paddy field, Critical Stages: Tillering, panicle initiation, and flowering
- **Fertilizer Strategy**: NPK recommendations: `N120-P60-K60 for irrigated HYV`
  *Schedule: Basal: 50%N+100%P+50%K; Topdressing: 25%N at tillering + 25%N at PI*
- **Harvesting**: Maturity indices: 85-90% grain golden yellow; moisture content 20-25%, Method: Reaping by sickle or combine harvester, Expected Yield: 3-8 t/ha (irrigated HYV); 1-2 t/ha (rainfed)

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB) (Bacterial)
- **Pathogen**: *Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked to yellowish stripes along leaf margins, turning white to grayish, 'Kresek' phase in young seedlings (complete wilting)
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Drain fields; copper hydroxide (2g/L) as weak bactericide spray
  * **Chemical**: Streptomycin + Tetracycline combination (limited effectiveness); Copper oxychloride preventively
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties (IR64, Swarna), avoid excessive nitrogen, drain infected fields, destroy stubble

#### Sheath Blight (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Rhizoctonia solani AG1-IA*
- **Symptoms**: Oval to elliptical greenish-gray lesions with white-gray interior and brownish border on leaf sheaths at water level
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Trichoderma harzianum or T. viride soil application (2.5 kg/ha), Pseudomonas fluorescens spray
  * **Chemical**: Hexaconazole 5% EC (1ml/L), Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Validamycin 3% L (2ml/L), Carbendazim (1g/L)
  * **Prevention**: Reduce plant density, lower nitrogen, drain standing water, use resistant varieties

#### Rice Blast (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Pyricularia oryzae (Magnaporthe oryzae)*
- **Symptoms**: Spindle-shaped (diamond) lesions on leaves with gray-white center and dark brown to reddish-brown margins
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Spray Pseudomonas fluorescens (1% w/v), seed treatment with Trichoderma, avoid excess nitrogen
  * **Chemical**: Tricyclazole 75% WP (0.6g/L), Isoprothiolane 40% EC (1.5ml/L), Kasugamycin 3% SL (1.5ml/L), Azoxystrobin
  * **Prevention**: Use blast-resistant varieties (BPT 5204, Swarna Sub1), balanced N fertilization, wider spacing for air circulation, destroy stubble

#### Brown Spot (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Helminthosporium oryzae (Bipolaris oryzae)*
- **Symptoms**: Small, oval to circular spots on leaves with yellow halo; dark brown margins and lighter centers
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Trichoderma seed treatment, adequate potassium nutrition
  * **Chemical**: Mancozeb 75% WP (2g/L), Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Iprodione (1.5g/L)
  * **Prevention**: Treat seeds with fungicide, correct soil nutrition (especially K and Si), use certified seeds, crop rotation

#### False Smut (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Ustilaginoidea virens*
- **Symptoms**: Individual spikelets converted to small velvet-like greenish balls
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Propiconazole-based sprays at booting stage
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L) or Azoxystrobin at booting stage
  * **Prevention**: Spray preventive fungicide at booting stage, avoid late planting, balanced nutrition

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Rice Weevil (*Sitophilus oryzae*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Grain kernels hollowed out; dust and frass in stored grain; characteristic exit holes; grain losses 20-40%; quality deterioration
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Clean dry grain storage (< 14% moisture); diatomaceous earth 1.5g/kg mixed with grain; bay leaves in storage; neem leaf powder mixed with grain; hermetic storage (PICS bags, metal silos)
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation (Aluminium phosphide tablets 3 tablets/tonne); Pyrethrin spray on empty stores; Deltamethrin WP on wall surfaces; Pirimiphos-methyl admixture
  * **Biological**: No practical biological control for storage pests

#### Fall Armyworm (*Spodoptera frugiperda*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Ragged windows in leaves; characteristic 'shot hole' damage in whorl; frass in leaf whorl; direct ear damage (in maize); can completely defoliate young plants
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Bt-kurstaki or Bt-aizawai spray; fall armyworm pheromone traps; sand+soil in whorl (suffocates larvae); egg mass and larvae hand collection
  * **Chemical**: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG (0.4g/L into whorl), Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen), Spinetoram, Lambda-cyhalothrin (adult moths)
  * **Biological**: Telenomus remus (egg parasitoid), Cotesia icipe, Coccygomimus turionellae, Metarhizium rileyi, NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus)

#### Yellow Stem Borer (Rice) (*Scirpophaga incertulas*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Deadheart: young plants central leaf whorl dies and turns brown (easy to pull out) — vegetative stage; Whitehead: panicle turns white and empty — reproductive stage
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Clip leaf tips of transplanted seedlings (removes egg masses); light traps; pheromone traps; release Trichogramma japonicum
  * **Chemical**: Cartap hydrochloride 4G granules (25 kg/ha in standing water), Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (2ml/L), Carbofuran 3G, Fipronil 0.3G
  * **Biological**: Trichogramma japonicum (egg parasitoid, 1 lakh/ha at 10-day intervals), Tetrastichus schoenobii, Telenomus rowani

#### Lesser Grain Borer (*Rhyzopertha dominica*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Flour dust from feeding activity; grain kernels bored from outside; 'rice dust'; severe infestations cause 30-50% losses; heat generation from infested grain
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Hermetic storage, diatomaceous earth, proper drying below 12% moisture
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation, Pirimiphos-methyl grain protectant
  * **Biological**: Limited; Theocolax elegans (ectoparasitoid wasp)

### 🌾 Crop Cultivation & Agronomy Guide

- **Origin**: Fertile Crescent (Middle East)
- **Climatic Preference**: Cool temperate to sub-tropical (Temp: Germination 3-25°C; optimal growth 15-21°C; grain fill 20-25°C, Rainfall: 375-875mm (rabi / winter crop; relies on residual moisture and irrigation))
- **Soil Requirements**: Well-drained fertile loam or clay-loam soils (pH: 6.0-7.5)
  *Special Note: Good aeration; moderate to high organic matter*
- **Sowing Details**: Seed rate: 100-125 kg/ha (timely sown); 125-150 kg/ha (late sown), Spacing: 22.5 cm row spacing (seed drill); broadcast is less preferred
- **Irrigation Needs**: Method: Flood/furrow; drip/sprinkler for water efficiency, Critical Stages: CRI (Crown Root Initiation at 21 DAS), Tillering, Jointing, Flowering, Grain filling
- **Fertilizer Strategy**: NPK recommendations: `N120-P60-K40 for HYV; N150 with S15 for better protein`
  *Schedule: Basal: 50%N+100%P+100%K; Topdressing: 25%N at CRI + 25%N at tillering*
- **Harvesting**: Maturity indices: Straw golden yellow; grain hard and moisture 18-20%, Method: Combine harvester or manual harvesting + threshing, Expected Yield: 3-6 t/ha irrigated; 1.5-2.5 t/ha rainfed

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Powdery Mildew (Wheat) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Blumeria graminis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: White fluffy powdery patches on upper leaf surface of lower leaves
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur (3g/L), potassium bicarbonate
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Triadimefon 25% WP (1g/L), Azoxystrobin
  * **Prevention**: Resistant varieties, avoid dense planting, balanced nitrogen, timely fungicide sprays

#### Stripe Rust (Yellow Rust) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Stripe of yellow-orange uredia arranged in rows along leaf veins on upper leaf surface
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur preventively; no effective organic cure
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Tebuconazole 250 EW (1ml/L), Azoxystrobin + Propiconazole
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties (HD-3226, WH1105), timely sowing to escape high-risk period, prophylactic fungicide at flag leaf stage

#### Loose Smut (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Ustilago tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Diseased plants emerge earlier; infected ear with black spore mass instead of grain
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Hot water seed treatment (52°C for 10 minutes)
  * **Chemical**: Carboxin + Thiram seed treatment (2g/kg seed), Tebuconazole seed treatment
  * **Prevention**: Use certified smut-free seeds, systemic seed treatment fungicides, plant resistant varieties

#### Stem Rust (Black Rust) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Puccinia graminis f. sp. tritici*
- **Symptoms**: Brick-red oval to elongated blisters (pustules) on stems and leaves that rupture releasing reddish-brown powdery spores
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur; remove barberry bushes from fields
  * **Chemical**: Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Tebuconazole (1ml/L), at first sign of infection
  * **Prevention**: Rust-resistant varieties, eradicate barberry, prophylactic fungicide in Ug99-risk areas

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Rice Weevil (*Sitophilus oryzae*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Grain kernels hollowed out; dust and frass in stored grain; characteristic exit holes; grain losses 20-40%; quality deterioration
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Clean dry grain storage (< 14% moisture); diatomaceous earth 1.5g/kg mixed with grain; bay leaves in storage; neem leaf powder mixed with grain; hermetic storage (PICS bags, metal silos)
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation (Aluminium phosphide tablets 3 tablets/tonne); Pyrethrin spray on empty stores; Deltamethrin WP on wall surfaces; Pirimiphos-methyl admixture
  * **Biological**: No practical biological control for storage pests

#### Lesser Grain Borer (*Rhyzopertha dominica*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Flour dust from feeding activity; grain kernels bored from outside; 'rice dust'; severe infestations cause 30-50% losses; heat generation from infested grain
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Hermetic storage, diatomaceous earth, proper drying below 12% moisture
  * **Chemical**: Phosphine fumigation, Pirimiphos-methyl grain protectant
  * **Biological**: Limited; Theocolax elegans (ectoparasitoid wasp)
