### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Diamondback Moth (*Plutella xylostella*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Windowing: larvae eat leaf tissue from below, leaving translucent papery epidermis on upper surface; total leaf skeletonization in heavy infestations; head borer
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Bacillus thuringiensis subsp. kurstaki (Bt-k) spray (most effective); neem seed kernel extract 5%; hand picking; pheromone traps for monitoring
  * **Chemical**: Spinosad 45% SC (0.3ml/L), Indoxacarb 14.5% SC (1ml/L), Chlorfluazuron (IGR), Emamectin benzoate 5% SG (0.2g/L), Flubendiamide
  * **Biological**: Cotesia plutellae (parasitic wasp), Diadegma semiclausum, Diadromus collaris; Bacillus thuringiensis

- **Pathogen**: *Sclerotinia sclerotiorum*

- **Treatment Protocols**:

### 🌾 Crop Cultivation & Agronomy Guide

- **Origin**: Andes Mountains, South America
- **Climatic Preference**: Cool temperate (Temp: 15-20°C for tuber initiation; growth 18-25°C, Rainfall: 500-700mm)
- **Soil Requirements**: Well-drained sandy loam or loam; deep friable soils (pH: 5.2-6.4 (avoids scab at lower pH))
  *Special Note: Deep tillage for loose soil for tuber expansion*
- **Sowing Details**: Seed rate: , Spacing: 60-75 cm × 15-20 cm (rows × plants)
- **Irrigation Needs**: Method: Furrow or drip irrigation, Critical Stages: Planting, tuber initiation (60-70 DAS), tuber bulking
- **Fertilizer Strategy**: NPK recommendations: `N180-P100-K200 (high K for quality)`
  *Schedule: All at planting or split with K*
- **Harvesting**: Maturity indices: Vine death; skin doesn't peel off when rubbed, Method: Mechanical digger or manual fork; avoid damage, Expected Yield: 20-40 t/ha

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Common Scab (Bacterial (Actinomycete))
- **Pathogen**: *Streptomyces scabies*
- **Symptoms**: No visible early symptoms on foliage
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Soil acidification (sulfur), crop rotation, adequate irrigation during tuber formation
  * **Chemical**: Quintozene PCNB seed treatment (limited), Flutolanil seed treatment
  * **Prevention**: Lower soil pH to 5.0-5.5, maintain soil moisture during tuber initiation, rotate with non-susceptible crops (cereals), use scab-resistant varieties

#### Late Blight (Potato) (Oomycete)
- **Pathogen**: *Phytophthora infestans*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked pale green spots on leaves, white sporulation on underside in humid conditions
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper hydroxide (2.5-3g/L) preventively, Bordeaux mixture, avoid excessive irrigation
  * **Chemical**: Metalaxyl-M + Mancozeb 2.5g/L, Dimethomorph + Mancozeb, Cymoxanil + Famoxadone, Fluopicolide
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties (Kufri Bahar, Jyoti), use certified disease-free seed tubers, apply fungicide before first rains, destroy volunteer plants

#### White Grubs (Chafer Beetles) (*Holotrichia consanguinea, Leucopholis lepidophora (multiple spp.)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Plants wilt suddenly (root destruction by grubs); patches of dead plants in field; soil becomes soft and easy to pull plants (roots eaten); adult beetles feed on tree leaves at night
- **IPM & Control Measures**:

  * **Biological**: Metarhizium anisopliae, Beauveria bassiana soil inoculants; Heterorhabditis bacteriophora (entomopathogenic nematode)

#### Leafhoppers (*Amrasca biguttula biguttula (Cotton/Okra Leafhopper); Empoasca spp.*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Cotton: 'Cotton leaf curl' or 'Jassid damage' — leaf edge curling upward, reddening; Potato/Brinjal: leaf hopper burn (hopperburn) — marginal leaf scorch and curling
- **IPM & Control Measures**:

  * **Biological**: Anagrus atomus (egg parasitoid in grapes), Gonatocerus ashmeadi, Stethorus ladybugs

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Fusarium Wilt (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. lycopersici*
- **Symptoms**: Yellowing and wilting of lower leaves starting on one side of the plant, often one branch first
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Trichoderma harzianum soil treatment, Solarization (clear polyethylene film over moist soil for 4-6 weeks)
  * **Chemical**: No effective chemical cure; soil fumigation with Metam sodium before planting as preventive
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties (race-specific), crop rotation (4+ years), avoid wounding roots, soil solarization

#### Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV) (Viral)
- **Pathogen**: *Tomato yellow leaf curl virus*
- **Symptoms**: Upward and inward curling of leaflets, yellowing at leaf margins
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: No cure once infected; control whitefly vector with neem oil, yellow sticky traps, reflective mulch
  * **Chemical**: Imidacloprid or Thiamethoxam for whitefly control. Mineral oil sprays to reduce whitefly transmission
  * **Prevention**: Plant TYLCV-resistant varieties, use insect-proof netting in nursery, roguing infected plants immediately, control whiteflies

#### Septoria Leaf Spot (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Septoria lycopersici*
- **Symptoms**: Small, circular water-soaked spots on lower leaves with dark brown margins and lighter gray-white centers with tiny black dots (pycnidia)
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper oxychloride 50% WP (3g/L), neem oil spray, remove infected lower leaves
  * **Chemical**: Chlorothalonil (2g/L), Mancozeb (2g/L), Azoxystrobin (0.5ml/L)
  * **Prevention**: Crop rotation, remove infected plant debris, avoid overhead irrigation, stake and prune for air circulation

#### Blossom End Rot (Physiological Disorder)
- **Pathogen**: *Calcium deficiency (Physiological)*
- **Symptoms**: Light brown water-soaked area at blossom end of green fruit
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Foliar calcium chloride spray (4g/L), consistent irrigation, mulching, agricultural lime in soil
  * **Chemical**: Calcium nitrate foliar spray (2g/L), drip application of calcium chelate
  * **Prevention**: Consistent irrigation schedule, mulch to retain soil moisture, soil pH 6.2-6.8, avoid excess ammonium nitrogen

#### Powdery Mildew (Tomato) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Leveillula taurica*
- **Symptoms**: Pale yellow spots on upper leaf surface, white powdery growth visible on underside
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur (3g/L), potassium bicarbonate (5g/L), neem oil, diluted milk (1:9)
  * **Chemical**: Hexaconazole (1ml/L), Propiconazole (1ml/L), Tebuconazole (1ml/L)
  * **Prevention**: Good air circulation, avoid dense planting, resistant varieties

#### Bacterial Wilt (Bacterial)
- **Pathogen**: *Ralstonia solanacearum*
- **Symptoms**: Sudden wilting of top leaves during hottest part of the day, recovery at night
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: No effective cure. Copper-based bactericides as weak suppressants
  * **Chemical**: No effective chemical treatment available
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties, crop rotation (minimum 3 years), soil solarization, avoid waterlogging, remove infected plants and soil, use clean tools

#### Root Knot Nematode (Nematode)
- **Pathogen**: *Meloidogyne incognita, M. javanica*
- **Symptoms**: Wilting during hot hours, poor plant vigor, chlorosis resembling nutrient deficiency
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Marigold intercropping (Tagetes sp.), neem cake soil application, Paecilomyces lilacinus, Pochonia chlamydosporia
  * **Chemical**: Carbofuran 3G (33kg/ha soil application), Ethoprophos 10G granules, Oxamyl
  * **Prevention**: Crop rotation with non-host crops (maize, cereals), soil solarization, use resistant varieties (Mi-gene)

#### Late Blight (Oomycete)
- **Pathogen**: *Phytophthora infestans*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked irregular grayish-green patches on leaves, usually starting from leaf tips and edges
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper hydroxide (3g/L) as protective spray, Bordeaux mixture (1%), maintain dry conditions
  * **Chemical**: Metalaxyl-M + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) 2.5g/L, Cymoxanil + Famoxadone 0.5g/L, Dimethomorph 0.5g/L
  * **Prevention**: Plant resistant varieties, ensure good drainage, avoid overhead irrigation, scout regularly during cool wet weather, destroy volunteer plants

#### Early Blight (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Alternaria solani*
- **Symptoms**: Small, dark brown circular spots with concentric rings (target board pattern) on older lower leaves
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Neem oil (4ml/L + 2ml soap), Copper oxychloride 50% WP (3g/L), Trichoderma harzianum soil drench, Baking soda spray (5g/L)
  * **Chemical**: Mancozeb 75% WP (2g/L), Azoxystrobin 23% SC (1ml/L), Chlorothalonil 75% WP (2g/L), Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L)
  * **Prevention**: Use certified disease-free seeds, crop rotation (3 years), remove infected debris, avoid overhead irrigation, stake plants for air circulation

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Whitefly (*Bemisia tabaci*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Yellowing leaves, sticky honeydew, sooty mold (secondary), vector of 200+ plant viruses (TYLCV, CLCuD, CBSD, cassava mosaic)
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Yellow sticky traps, neem oil + soap spray (5ml+2ml per L), reflective mulch, soap water spray, Beauveria bassiana
  * **Chemical**: Imidacloprid 17.8% SL (0.5ml/L), Thiamethoxam 25% WG (0.2g/L), Acetamiprid 20% SP (0.2g/L), Spiromesifen (resistance management)
  * **Biological**: Encarsia formosa (parasitic wasp), Eretmocerus mundus, Macrolophus pygmaeus (predatory bug), Beauveria bassiana

#### Fall Armyworm (*Spodoptera frugiperda*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Ragged windows in leaves; characteristic 'shot hole' damage in whorl; frass in leaf whorl; direct ear damage (in maize); can completely defoliate young plants
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Bt-kurstaki or Bt-aizawai spray; fall armyworm pheromone traps; sand+soil in whorl (suffocates larvae); egg mass and larvae hand collection
  * **Chemical**: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG (0.4g/L into whorl), Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen), Spinetoram, Lambda-cyhalothrin (adult moths)
  * **Biological**: Telenomus remus (egg parasitoid), Cotesia icipe, Coccygomimus turionellae, Metarhizium rileyi, NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus)

#### Cotton Bollworm (Helicoverpa) (*Helicoverpa armigera*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Entry holes in cotton bolls (with frass-filled tunnels), tomato fruit damage, chickpea pod damage with grain loss, maize kernel damage
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: NPV spray (2×10¹² POB/ha); HaNPV + cotton Bt; pheromone traps (1/ha); neem seed kernel extract; Bt-k spray
  * **Chemical**: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG (0.4g/L), Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC (0.3ml/L), Indoxacarb, Flubendiamide, Thiodicarb
  * **Biological**: Trichogramma chilonis (egg parasitoid, 1 lakh/ha weekly), Habrobracon hebetor, Chrysoperla carnea, HaNPV
