### 🌾 Crop Cultivation & Agronomy Guide

- **Origin**: New Guinea (Pacific Islands)
- **Climatic Preference**: Tropical and subtropical (Temp: 27-38°C growing season; 12-20°C for maturation and sucrose accumulation, Rainfall: 1500-2500mm; needs dry period for sucrose accumulation)
- **Soil Requirements**: Deep, well-drained alluvial or loamy soil (pH: 6.0-8.0)
- **Sowing Details**: Seed rate: , Spacing: 
- **Irrigation Needs**: Method: Drip irrigation most efficient; furrow also used, Critical Stages: Establishment, grand growth period (3-9 months)
- **Fertilizer Strategy**: NPK recommendations: `N280-P120-K160 planted crop; N240-P80-K140 ratoon`
  *Schedule: Basal: 50%P+50%K; N split every 45 days (5 splits)*
- **Harvesting**: Maturity indices: 12-14 months for plant crop; 10-12 months for ratoon; CCS (Commercial Cane Sugar) > 10%, Method: Manual harvesting (topped and detrashed) or mechanical harvester, Expected Yield: 70-100 t/ha cane; 8-11 t/ha sugar

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Fall Armyworm (*Spodoptera frugiperda*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Ragged windows in leaves; characteristic 'shot hole' damage in whorl; frass in leaf whorl; direct ear damage (in maize); can completely defoliate young plants
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Bt-kurstaki or Bt-aizawai spray; fall armyworm pheromone traps; sand+soil in whorl (suffocates larvae); egg mass and larvae hand collection
  * **Chemical**: Emamectin benzoate 5% SG (0.4g/L into whorl), Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen), Spinetoram, Lambda-cyhalothrin (adult moths)
  * **Biological**: Telenomus remus (egg parasitoid), Cotesia icipe, Coccygomimus turionellae, Metarhizium rileyi, NPV (Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus)

#### Yellow Stem Borer (Rice) (*Scirpophaga incertulas*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Deadheart: young plants central leaf whorl dies and turns brown (easy to pull out) — vegetative stage; Whitehead: panicle turns white and empty — reproductive stage
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Clip leaf tips of transplanted seedlings (removes egg masses); light traps; pheromone traps; release Trichogramma japonicum
  * **Chemical**: Cartap hydrochloride 4G granules (25 kg/ha in standing water), Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (2ml/L), Carbofuran 3G, Fipronil 0.3G
  * **Biological**: Trichogramma japonicum (egg parasitoid, 1 lakh/ha at 10-day intervals), Tetrastichus schoenobii, Telenomus rowani

#### White Grubs (Chafer Beetles) (*Holotrichia consanguinea, Leucopholis lepidophora (multiple spp.)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Plants wilt suddenly (root destruction by grubs); patches of dead plants in field; soil becomes soft and easy to pull plants (roots eaten); adult beetles feed on tree leaves at night
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Metarhizium anisopliae fungus (1×10⁹ spores/ml) soil application; ploughing to expose and destroy grubs; light traps for adults; neem cake 250 kg/ha
  * **Chemical**: Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (5L/ha) soil incorporation before planting; Carbofuran 3G (25 kg/ha); Phorate 10G; Imidacloprid seed treatment
  * **Biological**: Metarhizium anisopliae, Beauveria bassiana soil inoculants; Heterorhabditis bacteriophora (entomopathogenic nematode)
