## Apple

**Scientific Name**: *Malus domestica* Borkh.
**IUCN Conservation Status**: `LC`

### 📋 Botanical Taxonomy Hierarchy

| Rank | Name | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kingdom** | Plantae | Plant kingdom |
| **Division** | Tracheophyta | Vascular/Non-vascular division |
| **Class** | Magnoliopsida | Class classification |
| **Order** | Rosales | Order grouping |
| **Family** | Rosaceae | Rose family; includes apples, pears, cherries, plums, strawberries |
| **Genus** | Malus | Genus grouping |
| **Species** | *domestica* | Species epithet |

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Crown Gall (Bacterial)
- **Pathogen**: *Agrobacterium tumefaciens (Rhizobium radiobacter)*
- **Symptoms**: Small, soft, round galls develop at crown (soil line) or on roots
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Cut out galls; treat with Agrobacterium radiobacter strain K-84 (biocontrol) at planting
  * **Chemical**: No effective post-infection treatment; copper bactericides preventively
  * **Prevention**: Avoid wounding, use K-84 dip on roots before planting, plant only certified gall-free material

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Scale Insects (*Diaspidiotus perniciosus (San Jose Scale); Saissetia oleae (Olive Scale)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Yellowing, twig and branch death, reduced fruit quality, bark encrustation; honeydew (soft scales) leading to sooty mold
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Dormant oil spray (petroleum oil 3-5%) at crawler stage; white summer oil (1-2%); brush off with stiff brush
  * **Chemical**: Buprofezin (at crawler stage), Chlorpyrifos + oil, Spirotetramat systemic; dormant oil during winter
  * **Biological**: Encarsia perniciosi (parasitoid of San Jose scale), Comperiella bifasciata, Aphytis melinus (red scale parasitoid)

### 🌾 Crop Cultivation & Agronomy Guide

- **Origin**: Southeast Asia (Papua New Guinea region)
- **Climatic Preference**: Humid tropical (Temp: 26-30°C; growth stops below 15°C and above 38°C, Rainfall: 2000-2500mm; well distributed throughout year; critical to avoid dry spells)
- **Soil Requirements**: Deep, fertile, well-drained loam (pH: 5.5-7.0)

- **Fertilizer Strategy**: NPK recommendations: ``
- **Harvesting**: Maturity indices: , Method: Whole bunch harvested at once by cutting bunch stalk, Expected Yield: 40-60 t/ha (good management); 25-35 t/ha normal

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Panama Wilt (Fusarium Wilt of Banana) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. cubense (TR4)*
- **Symptoms**: Yellowing of older outer leaves, leaf collapse, starting from leaf margins; brown streaking in pseudostem when cut
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: No cure. Soil solarization, biological soil amendments (Trichoderma, compost)
  * **Chemical**: No effective chemical treatment
  * **Prevention**: Strict quarantine, plant resistant varieties (GCTCV-218 for TR4), clean tools with bleach, avoid infected soil movement

#### Black Sigatoka (Black Leaf Streak) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Pseudocercospora fijiensis*
- **Symptoms**: Tiny pale yellow flecks on lower leaf surface that elongate into brown streaks
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper oxychloride (2-3g/L) sprays, oil-based copper formulations; remove infected leaves
  * **Chemical**: Systemic fungicides on rotation: Propiconazole, Tebuconazole, Fenbuconazole, Chlorothalonil; resistance management essential
  * **Prevention**: Resistant varieties, remove lower infected leaves (deleafing), reduce humidity with good drainage and canopy management

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Fruit Fly (Oriental) (*Bactrocera dorsalis*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Pin-hole entry with sap/gum oozing; fruit surface discoloration, premature fruit drop; soft rotting interior with white maggots; secondary bacterial and fungal infections
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Male attractant traps (Methyl eugenol + malathion or GF-120); fruit bagging with paper or plastic bags; Protein bait sprays; pick and destroy infested fallen fruit
  * **Chemical**: Protein bait spray (GF-120 bait + Spinosad); Cover sprays Malathion 50% EC (2ml/L), Dimethoate (2ml/L); only during non-harvest period
  * **Biological**: Fopius arisanus (egg-larval parasitoid), Diachasmimorpha longicaudata, Fopius vandenboschi, mass rearing and release of sterile males (SIT)

## Grape

**Scientific Name**: *Vitis vinifera* L.
**IUCN Conservation Status**: `LC`

### 📋 Botanical Taxonomy Hierarchy

| Rank | Name | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kingdom** | Plantae | Plant kingdom |
| **Division** | Tracheophyta | Vascular/Non-vascular division |
| **Class** | Magnoliopsida | Class classification |
| **Order** | Vitales | Order grouping |
| **Family** | Vitaceae | Grape family |
| **Genus** | Vitis | Genus grouping |
| **Species** | *vinifera* | Species epithet |

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Crown Gall (Bacterial)
- **Pathogen**: *Agrobacterium tumefaciens (Rhizobium radiobacter)*
- **Symptoms**: Small, soft, round galls develop at crown (soil line) or on roots
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Cut out galls; treat with Agrobacterium radiobacter strain K-84 (biocontrol) at planting
  * **Chemical**: No effective post-infection treatment; copper bactericides preventively
  * **Prevention**: Avoid wounding, use K-84 dip on roots before planting, plant only certified gall-free material

#### Botrytis Gray Mold (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Botrytis cinerea*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked, brown lesions on petals, leaves, and stems; soft rot
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Remove infected tissue, improve air circulation, reduce humidity; Bacillus subtilis, Trichoderma spray
  * **Chemical**: Iprodione (1.5g/L), Pyrimethanil (1ml/L), Fenhexamid (1g/L), Cyprodinil (0.5g/L). Rotate fungicides for resistance management.
  * **Prevention**: Improve air circulation, avoid wounding, remove dead tissue, avoid waterlogging, maintain low humidity

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Mealybugs (*Phenacoccus solenopsis (Cotton Mealybug); Planococcus citri (Citrus Mealybug)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Yellowing, leaf drop, honeydew + sooty mold; cotton twisting; mango malformation spread; overall plant decline and death in severe infestations
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Neem oil + soap spray; rubbing alcohol on cotton swabs; strong water spray; remove waxy coating first; Cryptolaemus montrouzieri release
  * **Chemical**: Imidacloprid soil drench (2ml/L), Buprofezin 25% WP (1g/L), Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (2.5ml/L), Acetamiprid + Chlorpyrifos
  * **Biological**: Cryptolaemus montrouzieri (mealybug destroyer beetle), Leptomastix dactylopii (parasitoid wasp), Anagyrus kamali, Acerophagus papayae

#### Leafhoppers (*Amrasca biguttula biguttula (Cotton/Okra Leafhopper); Empoasca spp.*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Cotton: 'Cotton leaf curl' or 'Jassid damage' — leaf edge curling upward, reddening; Potato/Brinjal: leaf hopper burn (hopperburn) — marginal leaf scorch and curling
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Neem seed kernel extract 5%, neem oil; yellow sticky traps; Beauveria bassiana spray
  * **Chemical**: Imidacloprid 17.8% SL (0.3ml/L), Lambda-cyhalothrin 5% EC (1ml/L), Thiamethoxam, Dimethoate 30% EC (2ml/L)
  * **Biological**: Anagrus atomus (egg parasitoid in grapes), Gonatocerus ashmeadi, Stethorus ladybugs

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Powdery Mildew (Mango) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Oidium mangiferae*
- **Symptoms**: White powdery coating on young leaves, flower clusters, and young fruits
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Wettable sulfur 3g/L at bud break; diluted milk spray (1:5); potassium bicarbonate
  * **Chemical**: Triadimefon 25% WP (1g/L), Hexaconazole 5% EC (2ml/L), Propiconazole 25% EC (1ml/L), Myclobutanil
  * **Prevention**: First spray at bud emergence, second at fruit set; avoid overhead irrigation; morning sprays

#### Mango Malformation Disease (MMD) (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Fusarium mangiferae*
- **Symptoms**: Vegetative malformation: bunchy top appearance with compacted proliferation of shoots ('mango witches' broom')
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Remove and burn malformed parts; control mites with acaricides
  * **Chemical**: NAA (Naphthaleneacetic acid) 200ppm spray + Carbendazim (0.1%) at panicle emergence
  * **Prevention**: Use disease-free planting material, certified nursery plants, deinfest cutting tools, control mite vectors

#### Anthracnose (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides*
- **Symptoms**: Dark brown to black spots on young leaves, flowers, and immature fruits; 'blossom blight' killing flower clusters
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Copper oxychloride 3g/L, neem oil + copper, hot water dip 52°C for 5 minutes (post-harvest)
  * **Chemical**: Carbendazim 0.1% + Mancozeb 0.25% spray, Azoxystrobin + Difenoconazole, Propiconazole 0.1%
  * **Prevention**: Prune dead wood, copper spray before flowering, proper post-harvest handling (hot water), waxing, cold chain

### 🐛 Entomological Threats (Pests)

#### Mealybugs (*Phenacoccus solenopsis (Cotton Mealybug); Planococcus citri (Citrus Mealybug)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Yellowing, leaf drop, honeydew + sooty mold; cotton twisting; mango malformation spread; overall plant decline and death in severe infestations
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Neem oil + soap spray; rubbing alcohol on cotton swabs; strong water spray; remove waxy coating first; Cryptolaemus montrouzieri release
  * **Chemical**: Imidacloprid soil drench (2ml/L), Buprofezin 25% WP (1g/L), Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (2.5ml/L), Acetamiprid + Chlorpyrifos
  * **Biological**: Cryptolaemus montrouzieri (mealybug destroyer beetle), Leptomastix dactylopii (parasitoid wasp), Anagyrus kamali, Acerophagus papayae

#### Fruit Fly (Oriental) (*Bactrocera dorsalis*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Pin-hole entry with sap/gum oozing; fruit surface discoloration, premature fruit drop; soft rotting interior with white maggots; secondary bacterial and fungal infections
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Male attractant traps (Methyl eugenol + malathion or GF-120); fruit bagging with paper or plastic bags; Protein bait sprays; pick and destroy infested fallen fruit
  * **Chemical**: Protein bait spray (GF-120 bait + Spinosad); Cover sprays Malathion 50% EC (2ml/L), Dimethoate (2ml/L); only during non-harvest period
  * **Biological**: Fopius arisanus (egg-larval parasitoid), Diachasmimorpha longicaudata, Fopius vandenboschi, mass rearing and release of sterile males (SIT)

#### Mango Stone Weevil (*Sternochetus mangiferae*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: No external fruit damage visible; seed destroyed; premature fruit drop; larvae tunnel through cotyledons; affects seed viability for propagation
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Collect and destroy fallen infested fruits; tree hygiene — remove old bark; hot water seed treatment for nursery use (52°C for 15 min)
  * **Chemical**: Chlorpyrifos 20% EC (2ml/L) spray when adults are active (Feb-March); trunk banding with insecticide strips
  * **Biological**: No effective commercial biological control

#### Scale Insects (*Diaspidiotus perniciosus (San Jose Scale); Saissetia oleae (Olive Scale)*)
- **Damage Symptoms**: Yellowing, twig and branch death, reduced fruit quality, bark encrustation; honeydew (soft scales) leading to sooty mold
- **IPM & Control Measures**:
  * **Organic/IPM**: Dormant oil spray (petroleum oil 3-5%) at crawler stage; white summer oil (1-2%); brush off with stiff brush
  * **Chemical**: Buprofezin (at crawler stage), Chlorpyrifos + oil, Spirotetramat systemic; dormant oil during winter
  * **Biological**: Encarsia perniciosi (parasitoid of San Jose scale), Comperiella bifasciata, Aphytis melinus (red scale parasitoid)

### 🦠 Pathological Diagnosis (Diseases)

#### Botrytis Gray Mold (Fungal)
- **Pathogen**: *Botrytis cinerea*
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked, brown lesions on petals, leaves, and stems; soft rot
- **Treatment Protocols**:
  * **Organic**: Remove infected tissue, improve air circulation, reduce humidity; Bacillus subtilis, Trichoderma spray
  * **Chemical**: Iprodione (1.5g/L), Pyrimethanil (1ml/L), Fenhexamid (1g/L), Cyprodinil (0.5g/L). Rotate fungicides for resistance management.
  * **Prevention**: Improve air circulation, avoid wounding, remove dead tissue, avoid waterlogging, maintain low humidity
